Victory day: 16th December

A short description about the significance of the day by Mahathir M Lutfullah

What was achieved on this day

The Bangladesh Independence War in 1971 was a conflict between East and West Pakistan, leading to the creation of the independent state of Bangladesh. Fueled by political and economic disparities, the war resulted in widespread atrocities, with Bangladesh emerging victorious and gaining sovereignty on December 16, 1971.

This day was later coined the Victory Day, with Bangladesh celebrating it with passion every year.

Fact sheet about the war

  • Background: East and West Pakistan (now Bangladesh and Pakistan) were geographically separated, with cultural and linguistic differences exacerbating tensions.
  • Causes: Economic and political disparities, language issues, and a desire for autonomy fueled demands for an independent Bangladesh.
  • Timeline: March 26, 1971, marked the start of the conflict. The war lasted for approximately nine months, culminating in victory for Bangladesh on December 16, 1971.
  • Atrocities: Widespread human rights abuses, including mass killings, rape, and displacement, were perpetrated by the Pakistani military.
  • International Response: The conflict garnered global attention, with India playing a crucial role in supporting Bangladesh. The international community condemned the violence and recognized Bangladesh's independence.
  • Humanitarian Impact: The war resulted in a significant humanitarian crisis, with millions displaced and a high death toll.
  • Outcome: Bangladesh emerged as an independent nation, with victory celebrated on December 16, 1971.
  • Legacy: The war left a lasting impact on the region, shaping the identity of Bangladesh and influencing geopolitics in South Asia. The scars of the conflict persist in collective memory.
